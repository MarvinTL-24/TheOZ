D) Core System: "Chained System"

Chained System - Core Mechanic

a. Overview

The Chained System is the core action mechanic that runs through both combat and exploration in The Ashen OZ.

Using "Chains," Dorothy can impale enemies to initiate combat, or embed chains into Dreamcatchers (＝Anchors) to propel herself through space.

By literally stitching together fragments of a broken world, the system organically fuses combat and traversal into a single flow.

b. System Structure

The Chained System consists of two primary axes:

Chained Attack

Drive a chain into an enemy to dash forward and initiate combat. The ignition point of the combat loop

Chained Move (Dreamcatcher)

Embed chains into anchors to move, re-jump, or reposition. The driving force of map traversal

c. Core Philosophy

To address a common issue in Metroidvania games— the separation and disruption between combat and exploration— the Chained System is built upon the following design principles:

Continuity

Combat and movement are unified into a single input flow. After engaging an enemy with a chained dash, the same input can be used to wall-traverse or immediately re-engage another target. There is no rhythmic break between combat, platforming, and exploration.

Momentum

The Chained System is not merely "fast." It delivers speed with rhythm and weight, creating a distinct sense of momentum and action satisfaction.

Metroidvania Differentiation

The Chained System creates a unique gameplay identity that distinguishes The Ashen OZ from traditional Metroidvanias, offering seamless integration of combat and exploration.

E) The Ashen Die

"Dorothy picked up an old die amid the ruins of the orphanage."

A hurricane erupted where the die struck the ground, pulling her into the world of Oz. Each face of the die was engraved with six remnants— the fundamental forces that govern this world.

The Ashen Die - Core Mechanic

a) System Overview

  • Structured similarly to Hades' 'Boons of the Gods.'
  • After combat rewards, memory fractures, or specific events, players may roll the Ashen Dice.
  • Choose one of three faces and assign it to a skill slot (jump, attack, chained action, soul pact, etc.).
  • Each face has its own unique attributes, effects, and visual themes.

  • Five total tiers: Common, Rare, Special, Heroic, Legendary

F) Random-Room Combat

"Every run delivers different battles, different enemies, and varied map layouts— forming the foundation of the meta-roguelite system."

a) Combat Room

Purpose: Fast, exhilarating combat experience + Emotion Gauge accumulation

Combat Flow: Entry → Small enemy waves → Elite or Mini-Boss

b) Exploration Room

Purpose: Relieve combat fatigue, environmental exploration, discovery of hidden paths and items

Features: Short platforming puzzles, Chain-based movement sections, Resource nodes

c) Event Room

Purpose: Narrative progression, emotional choices, risk–reward decisions (buffs vs. penalties)

Features a choice-driven dialogue interface

d) Mini-Boss Room

Purpose: Themed mini-boss encounters / narrative linkage before unlocking companions

Combat Structure: Two-phase fight
Phase 1: Standard patterns
Phase 2: Emotional Overdrive

Mini-Boss Variations

Unique encounters with thematic bosses tied to the story progression

Companion Unlocks

Defeating mini-bosses unlocks new companions and abilities

Soul Pact System

Gain access to companion abilities through mini-boss victories

e) Boss Room

Purpose: Advance the main story, emotional purification, companion unlocks
Features: 2–3 phase boss battles, Post-battle purification cutscene, Main story progression tied directly into the loop system

Monster Type Classification

No. Type Primary Role Combat Style / AI Behavior
1️⃣ Berserker Frontline Pressure Charges straight at the player; collides with walls to self-stun. Breaks through attacks with super armor.
